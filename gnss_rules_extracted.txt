2 Detailed Problem Statement – GNSS Anti-Spoofing
2.1 Background and Context
Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS) form the backbone of modern navigation,
timing, and synchronization across:
•Aviation
•Maritime navigation
•Autonomous drones
•Logistics
•Telecommunications
•Financial systems
•National infrastructure
GNSS signals are inherently low-power and broadcast in open environments, making them
vulnerable to spoofing attacks.
Spoofing involves transmitting manipulated signals that cause receivers to compute incorrect
position, velocity, or timing information. Such attacks may lead to navigation failures,
safety risks, financial losses, and infrastructure instability.
2.2 Core Problem Definition
Design and develop an AI-driven solution capable of detecting GNSS spoofing attempts
by analyzing signal behavior, temporal patterns, and inconsistencies over time.
Solutions must demonstrate:
•Practical feasibility
•Robustness
•Generalization capability
•Clear engineering reasoning
The focus is strictly on detection and resilience, not on generating spoofing attacks.
2.3 Expected Capabilities
The proposed solution should:
1

•Analyze GNSS signal characteristics and temporal dynamics
•Identify anomalies indicative of spoofing
•Differentiate genuine signals from manipulated signals
•Operate robustly across datasets and conditions
•Demonstrate detection through a prototype or evaluable system
2.4 Permitted Technical Approaches
Participants may explore:
Machine Learning Techniques
•Support Vector Machines (SVM)
•Random Forests
•Classical anomaly detection models
Deep Learning Approaches
•CNNs on raw signals or spectrograms
•LSTM / Transformer-based temporal models
Hybrid Approaches
•Classical signal processing (frequency-domain, wavelets, correlator analysis)
•Combined AI/ML architectures
2.5 Evaluation Philosophy
Solutions will be evaluated on:
•Generalization across datasets
•Fairness and absence of data leakage
•Robustness to distribution shifts
•Clarity of documentation and research thinking

6 Evaluation Strategy
The dataset is a classification problem and contains imbalanced classes.
Round 1 Evaluation Metric
Primary evaluation metric:
Weighted F1 Score
4

Weighted F1 is used to fairly evaluate performance across imbalanced classes and prevent
bias toward majority class predictions.
Submissions will also be checked for:
•Data leakage
•Invalid format
•Hardcoded predictions
Round 2 and Round 3 Evaluation
Composite evaluation based on:
•Quantitative model performance
•Generalization ability
•Robustness under updated constraints
•Documentation quality
•Research depth and first-principle reasoning
•Presentation clarity
